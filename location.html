FACTS & FIGURES

  • Brisbane is the capital city of the state of Queensland and is the third most populous city in Australia.
  • Despite ranking third in population, Brisbane is the largest of Australia's six capital cities by geographic area comprising 15,842 km2 (in the greater brisbane area).
  • Brisbane is situated on the eastern seaboard of Australia with the city centre positioned along the Brisbane River.
  • The Brisbane River is otherwise known as the 'Brown Snake' owing to its snaking shape and water colour.
      This is also a reference to the Eastern Brown Snake, native to Australia and a common sight around Brisbane. The Eastern Brown has the second most toxic venom of all snakes worldwide, after the Inland Taipan which is also native to Australia.
  • The population as of 2023 was 2,706,966, with a density of 159 people per km2.
  • Brisbane was established in May of 1825, 199 years ago. The exact date of establishment is unknown.
  • Brisbane is colloquially known as the "Sunshine State" due to the abundant blue skies and pleasant year round temperatures.
  • In 2015, a competition by travel guidebook Rough Guide elected Brisbane as one of the top ten most beautiful cities in the world.
      • Rough Guide cited reasons for the nomination as: "its winning combination of high-rise modern architecture, lush green spaces and the enormous Brisbane River that snakes its way through the centre before emptying itself into the azure Moreton Bay"
